02 — Public / Private Zoning

Zoning logic & execution

The plan does NOT achieve a clean public/private split. M-BEDROOM 1 (master) sits on the top-left — the same side as the Living/Dining and Open Kitchen. The left side is a conflict zone, not a coherent public or private zone. Bedrooms 2 and 3 occupy the right side only. There is no unified bedroom wing.

03 — Circulation Analysis

The critical failure

Confirmed by the developer's own plan annotation: 27.29 m² of circulation. This is the single largest planning deficiency in this layout.

Circulation area: 27.29 m² = 17.9% of total BUA
17.9%
Standard residential planning targets maximum 10–12% circulation. This layout exceeds that by approximately 9 m² of dead corridor. At AED 2.5M, with a typical JVC price of approximately AED 1,524/sqft, those 9 m² (96.9 sqft) represent approximately AED 147,000 of purchased area you cannot live in. This is a direct financial inefficiency embedded in the developer's design.
Central spine does not activate
~12m
The central horizontal corridor connects the living wing to the bedroom wing across approximately 12 metres of plan depth. None of this passage is converted to productive use. A well-designed plan would activate the corridor with linen storage, display niches, a compact pantry, or a study nook — reducing perceived circulation while adding function.
Apartment will feel like a corridor unit
At 17.9% circulation, the lived experience will be defined by movement rather than comfort. Occupants travelling from the master bedroom (far left) to bedroom 3 (far right) cross the full length of the apartment multiple times daily. This is a practical livability issue beyond the plan data. Interior design can mitigate perception but cannot fix the geometry.
Circulation: Poor to Average — Main planning weakness

06 — Open Kitchen Analysis

Labeled open, functions as a galley

Confirmed dimensions: 2,920mm × 1,700mm = 4.96 m². Counter depth confirmed at 600mm → clear working passage: 1,100mm. Net area of 4.96 m² falls below the DBC minimum of 5.0 m² for a kitchen. The developer markets this as an "Open Kitchen" — neither the area nor the layout supports that label.

4.96 m² — below DBC minimum · Single-wall galley only
4.96 m²
Confirmed: 2,920mm × 1,700mm = 4.96 m². DBC minimum kitchen area is 5.0 m² — this kitchen falls 0.04 m² below the legal minimum. Counter depth confirmed at 600mm → clear working passage = 1,100mm. While 1,100mm allows a single person to work, it is below the 1,200mm comfort standard. Two people cannot pass simultaneously. No island is geometrically possible. The entire counter run is approximately 2.3m effective — insufficient for a 3BR family kitchen.
"Open Kitchen" label is commercially misleading
At 4.96 m² with a single counter wall and 1,100mm passage, this is a compact galley kitchen, not an open-plan kitchen. A 3BR family apartment at AED 2.5M should deliver ≥ 9 m² with: working passage ≥ 1,200mm, counter run ≥ 3.0m, breakfast bar or island capability, and a separate pantry zone. None of these are achievable in the confirmed 2,920 × 1,700mm footprint. The "Open Kitchen" designation refers to the open visual connection to the living area — not to kitchen size or function.

Overall space planning

This layout is functional but inefficient. It delivers three bedrooms, three ensuites, a generous balcony, and clear public/private separation. For a buyer who values bedroom count, bathroom provision, and outdoor space, the plan is liveable and above average in those specific categories.

However, the plan is not a strong use of 1,640 square feet. The developer has made structural choices — an elongated plan form, a narrow kitchen, a wide circulation spine, and a left-side corridor that serves both the master bedroom and guest access to the living zone simultaneously — that cannot be resolved through interior design. M-BEDROOM 1 is acoustically exposed to both guest corridor traffic and kitchen proximity noise. The separation of Bedroom 1 from Bedrooms 2 and 3 is not inherently negative — for a family, this configuration gives parents acoustic distance from children — but the shared guest/private corridor on the left side remains an unresolved privacy deficiency.

The circulation figure of 27.29 m² (17.9%) is the clearest evidence of this. It is not estimated — it is printed on the developer's own plan. That single data point confirms that this layout was designed to maximise saleable metrics (bedroom count, bathroom count, balcony size) rather than to maximise livability quality.

Professional recommendation: Good enough to function, not good enough to be called a strong layout. Before committing at AED 2.5M, compare with at least two other 3BR units in JVC or JLT at the same price point. Request the area breakdown sheet from each developer — if the competing unit's circulation is below 18 m², it is a more efficient buy for the same money. If this unit is the preferred choice, the fit-out brief should prioritise corridor activation (storage/display), kitchen expansion where structurally possible, and a defined entry zone.
